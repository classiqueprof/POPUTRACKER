    What is a PIN?


    A Population Identification Number (PIN) is a Unique, Lifelong number
    Assigned to every individual at birth or registration. It serves as a digital
    identity for accurate population tracking.

    How is the PIN Assigned?


    A PIN is generated at birth and updated throughout an individual's life. It is
    linked to key life events such as birth registration, migration, and death
    records

    Why is the PIN important


    The PIN ensures accurate population data, eliminates census
    inefficiencies, and enables better planning for resource allocation,
    governance, and public services.

    What information is linked to the PIN?


    Each PIN is associated with vital records like birth and death certificates,
    migration history, residency status, and demographic details.

    How Does the PIN Improve Governance?


    With real-time data, government agencies can make informed decisions
    about infrastructure, healthcare, education, and economic planning.
    ensuring efficient resource distribution.

    About the Population and Population Estimates

    The Nigeria population clock is based on a series of short term projections for the resident population of Nigeria. This
    includes people whose usual residence is in the 36 states and Federal Capital Territory Abuja. These projections do
    not include members of the Armed Forces overseas, their dependents, or other Nigeria citizens residing outside the
    country.

    The projects are based on a monthly series of population estimates from 2006 population census. At the end of each
    year, a revised series of population estimates from the census date forward is used to update the short term
    projections for the population clock. Once the updated series of monthly projections is completed, the daily
    population clock values are derived by interpolation. Within each calendar month, the daily numerical population
    change is assumed to be constant, subject to negligible differences caused by rounding.

    Population estimates are gotten from National Bureau Statistics, Nigeria Population Commission, Immigration
    services, Hospitals, etc.
